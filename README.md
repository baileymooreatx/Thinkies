# Introduction

**[Thinkies](https://thinkies.org/)** are a collection of over **90 pattern-based thinking habits**
developed by Kent Beck to help people generate ideas and break through stuck
thinking. Each Thinkie consists of a recognizable **pattern** (a situation) and
a specific **transformation** (an operation) to apply when that pattern is
encountered, grounded in the principle of **deferred judgment**—separating idea
generation from evaluation.

## Key aspects of the Thinkies framework

* **Core Examples**: Common transformations include
    * "Can't/Because" (turning "We can't X because Y" into "When not Y, then we can X")
    * "Easy Bit" (identifying the smallest helpful action when paralyzed by a large problem).
* **Categories**: The patterns are organized into areas such as
    * getting unstuck
    * decision-making
    * problem reframing
    * system design
    * process optimization
* **Community & Events**: The Thinkies community holds periodic virtual events
  where practitioners share and develop new patterns.
  * **[Thinkies World Congress](https://tidyfirst.substack.com/p/thinkie-world-congress-1)** was held on June 4, 2025,
    exclusively for paying subscribers of Beck’s Software Design: Tidy First? newsletter.
  * **[Thinkies World Congress II](https://thinkies.org/congress/twc-2/)** scheduled for May 20, 2026
* **Distribution**: Beck publishes Thinkies on his Substack newsletter, **Tidy First?**, where paid subscribers receive
  a new Thinkie each week along with early access to conference content.
* **Goal**: The system aims to answer the frequent question "How did you think of that?" by providing learnable tricks
  of thought that make creative problem-solving more accessible.

## Thinkie patterns

Kent Beck has not publicly released a single, complete list of all 90+ Thinkie
patterns. The full catalog is **exclusively available to paid subscribers** of
his [Tidy First?](https://tidyfirst.substack.com/) Substack newsletter, where
he shares one new Thinkie per week. However, several specific Thinkies have been 
shared publicly in articles, blog posts, and his writing.

* **1, 10, or 100?** (also called **Orders of Magnitude**): When faced with a 
  vague estimate, clarify the scale by offering options that differ by orders 
  of magnitude (e.g., "Is that an hour, a day, or a week?").  
* **But If You Could...**: When something seems impossible, ask what would 
  happen if it were possible, and explore what's stopping you.  
* **Can't/Because**: Transform "We can't X because Y" into "When not Y, then we can X."  
* **Easy Bit**: When paralyzed by a large problem, identify and do the smallest, 
  most helpful action.  
* **Fun Bit**: When stuck, pick the most enjoyable piece of the problem to work on.  
* **Half Wavelength Shift**: In a multistep process, try skipping some initial 
  steps (a principle behind TDD).  
* **Latency, Throughput, & Variance**: When asked to "go faster," determine if
  the need is for more throughput, less latency, or lower variance.  
* **Naive Solution**: When stuck on a complicated solution, ask why the problem 
  is harder than a naive solution.  
* **One Pass Quickly, One Pass Good**: Deliver a scaled-down version of everything
  first, then go back and improve it.
* **Separate Observation From Judgement**: Distinguish between what you see and 
  the conclusions you draw from it.
* **Survivable?**: If you can survive the negative outcome of a decision, act
  in the cheapest, fastest way to generate value. Otherwise, mitigate the risk.
* **Win/Win**: Make a decision that is good for everyone involved.
* **Work Backwards**: Start at the desired end state and work your way back to 
  your current position.
